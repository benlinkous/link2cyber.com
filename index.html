// who-is-this-for

Built for real people starting from zero.

This bootcamp grew out of a simple conviction: the best cybersecurity training shouldn't cost thousands of dollars or require a CS degree. It's free, structured, and built to be followed — not just browsed.

  • Career changers who want to break into IT and cyber
  • Veterans and military families transitioning to civilian tech
  • Students in NRV / SWVA looking for a real cert path
  • Self-taught learners who need structure, not just YouTube rabbit holes
  • Anyone who wants CompTIA certs and doesn't know where to start
Open the Curriculum →
⚙️

What you'll need to start

  • A computer — Windows, Mac, or Linux all work
  • A free TryHackMe account (tryhackme.com)
  • Obsidian for note-taking (free download)
  • LinksOS or any Debian-based VM for labs (linksos.org)
  • Git and a free GitHub account
No paid tools required.
📅

How to pace yourself

  • Full-time learner: 8–10 hrs/day, 80 days
  • Part-time: 2–3 hrs/day, ~6 months
  • Weekend warrior: flexible — each day stands alone
  • All content is public — jump in on any day
// about

Built by Ben Linkous.

Retired Army Master Sergeant. Pulaski, Virginia. Planning Commission member. I spent 20+ years in the military and came out convinced that the skills that protect people — in cyber, in leadership, in community — should be free and accessible to anyone willing to do the work.

This bootcamp is the resource I wish had existed when I transitioned. Every day is structured the way I'd want to be taught: clear objectives, hands-on labs, real documentation, no wasted time.

Link2Cyber is part of a 22-site network called LINKS — built around education, civic life, and family legacy in the New River Valley.
